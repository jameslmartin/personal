Field notes on cleaning up URLs

15 Feb. 2015

So today I wanted to set to cleaning up the URLs for the site. This is a super simple hack, does not take long to complete, and makes your URLs much prettier. My roommate, Chris Hendel (@chris_hendel), does professional web development for Pebble. We discuss quality design in web development quite frequently, and while he is much better than I am with it, we'll often chat about the latest tools and frameworks. I asked him how to change my code so that the filenames do not appear at the end of my URLs, and he told me this is all you have to do:

  1. Make folders for each route of your website
  2. Rename the HTML file of each route to be index.html
  3. Change all the links on your site to open the directory of the route

For example, if I wanted to go to my about page from my landing page, the link in the a tag would just be:

about/

This way, in the URL bar we see:

cs.unc.edu/~jamesml/about/

...instead of the clunky:

cs.unc/edu/~jamesml/about.html (or) cs.unc.edu/~jamesml/about/index.html

You just have to make sure you edit all the links in your web pages to avoid 404s. My next hack may be making a small Python script to compile .ipynbs to HTML with better CSS styling. I also may look into a way of serving my posts rather than uploading static web pages.
